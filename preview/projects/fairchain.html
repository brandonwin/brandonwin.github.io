In an increasingly online art world, Fairchain seeks to build a more equitable relationship between artists and collectors through granting royalties on subsequent sales to artists.

However, the reality is that the art world is slow and resistant to change. However with a great user experience, Fairchain can change the norm and create a more sustainable ecosystem for artists and collectors alike.

Problem

Fairchain has complex contracts that may alienate potential customers and delay the signing process.

Solution

Build a cohesive design system that sends a clear message to users on how to smoothly complete a Fairchain contract.

Setting pixels straight

I realized the need early on for a robust design system that included desktop and mobile designs. Fairchain was in a period of rapid growth product-wise, where a new feature was being released every week. I took the initiative to create a design system that kept coherency across all platforms and stated guidelines for future releases.

Cohesion across platforms

As new features were being added and existing ones being updated, I created guidelines relating to margins and padding for all components since a system had not been put in place prior. I took an iterative approach to this system, developing it incrementally as I learned more about the app's backend.

Research

Users want to be highly informed on Fairchain contracts and their finer details but also keep it simple.
Users want to be frequently updated on the status of their contracts.
Users want to be convinced (and convince others) that Fairchain is the modern approach to the art world.

Direction

Provide clarity through callouts and interfaces that better inform the user.
Design additional ways to mitigate user confusion, such as push notifications and a site, to better explain Fairchain.

User Persona

Richard, 32

Richard is an experienced gallerist who is introduced to Fairchain by an artist who's in his upcoming gallery show. He loves the idea of Fairchain, but he's daunted by certain pain points and potential complications with the Fairchain contract.

Motivations

To have clarity on how Fairchain works for artists, galleries, collectors and other parties.
To seamlessly integrate Fairchain into current workflows.
"Fairchain is a great concept, but I'm worried about implementing it with other gallerists, collectors, and ultimately the artist."

Learnings

  1. Accounting for every audience is a challenge. Fairchain is a platform made for the industry as a whole—artists, galleries, collectors, advisors and estates. I learned to design for every edge case, and I adapted marketing resources to better target each audience.
  2. Communication with different stakeholders is key. As the sole designer, I worked with PMs and engineers to simultaneously deliver different product features and make sure nothing gets lost design-wise.
  3. Acting as the sole product designer is a role with many hats. I had to juggle different projects, manage my deadlines, reprioritize tasks, and design a website in the middle of all of that.
