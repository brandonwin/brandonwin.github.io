Pitch deck

Getting furniture online can be exciting, challenging and daunting at the same time.

For college students who are moving out for the first time, purchasing furniture is a huge headache. Furniture might not be what you want, it might not look the way you want, and it might not even arrive at your doorstep. FurnishStore seeks to solve that.

Problem

College students need a platform for purchasing affordable furniture and getting it delivered. Local businesses also need a platform that will connect them to potential customers.

Solution

Build a digital platform to deliver local furniture to the customer.

Understanding the user

My primary audience was college students ready to move out of their homes or dorms, some for the very first time. I wanted to make the interface feel easy to navigate. I also designed a "Collections" page since in my research, I found that the user cares as much about the look and design of the product as the budget and functionality.

Picture-book design

Users are here to shop for furniture, not read the terms and conditions. In my designs, I chose to make the screens be taken up mostly by pictures, with the necessary information (price, name, material) in large font.

Research

Users were invested in supporting local businesses.
Users wanted to create/thrift their own interior styles.
Users want to be informed on the delivery status and certain of how they'll receive them.

Direction

Emphasize supporting local businesses through design and callouts.
Methodically distill information through hierarchy and spacing.
Design a experience that clearly shows each part of the purchasing process.

User Persona

Neha, 20

Neha is a college student preparing to move out of her dorm and start living in her own apartment. She wants to build her own style, but she's worried about logistics and buying from places other than IKEA.

Motivations

To know where, when and how is her furniture going to be delivered.
To build and develop her own style.
"I want to buy furniture and have my own style but I don't know where it's going to be during delivery or when it's going to get here. Why can't this be like Amazon or Doordash?"

Learnings

  1. Pitching an idea is meant to hook people in, much like a short story. I regularly found myself iterating on my pitch and getting to the "kicker", my nickname for the major part of the pitch.
  2. Experienced colleagues and gurus are your best allies. Every UX standard is tough to know for a junior designer, but experienced designers spot the biggest improvements quickly and offer tools to do so.
  3. Source design feedback from different disciplines. Design is a universal language, and learning from different disciplines helped me think innovatively on FurnishStore.
