heebiekeebies aims to swiftly deliver mechanical keyboard parts at a bargain price in a growing market.

As a local business that originally conducted off of forums and a Discord server, heebiekeebies is looking to transition to Shopify to attract more potential customers and grow their brand.

Problem

heebiekeebies needs to build an ecommerce site to scale their business and brand.

Solution

Build a Shopify site that accomodates all parts of the shopping process, from product selection to delivery, for new and existing customers.

Brand voice and vision

While conducting market analysis, I found that many keyboard sites fell into many design potholes, whether they were hard to navigate, very expensive, or just looked plain boring. I chose to make the brand voice feel approachable and casual, but knowledgeable and money-smart. As for the vision, I wanted to make the site look easy on the eyes and informative, but not overwhelming with the information that the user may see.

Research

Catch customer attention quickly. Many keyboard enthusiasts purchase from different businesses based on word of mouth.
Existing customers will want a smooth transition to a website from using the heebiekeebies Discord server.
An attractive brand look will draw customers who want an attractive but highly functional product.

Direction

Create a detailed project list of necessary tasks leading up to launch.
Design a unique brand look that will set the business apart through copywriting and visual identity.
Launching QoL features to add to the customer process to improve the user experience for customers.

User Persona

Clarence, 24

Clarence is looking for new additions to his mechanical keyboard collection. He's a burgeoning enthusiast but he's not looking to break his bank, and he's also worried about buying from smaller vendors.

Motivations

To expand his keyboard collection with quality products while saving money.
To feel secure with his purchasing.
"I'm really into mechanical keyboards, but so many sites are expensive and take way too long to ship my purchases. This hobby is getting popular, but why doesn't the convenience factor catch up?"

Keycaps galore

I was given a large product catalogue that would be available on launch, and I designed the information architecture for which categories these products would fall into. All products were organized with tags in the backend for my client to manage after my contract expired.

Brands and standards

I collaborated with heebiekeebies's visual designer to create a coherent style and branding for the site. I advocated for the user during the design process, making sure that the website met accessibility standards with colorblindness, usability and screen readers. Additionally, I designed style guides for CTAs that would help navigate new users to newer products or products that were on sale.

Learnings

  1. Transparency and constant feedback with the client is core. As this was my first large design contract, I learned how to conduct feedback review within a client-designer relationship.
  2. There's always something new to learn in web design. This was my first foray into backend development, and learning the Shopify API was a challenge that I overcame.
  3. Interactions between designers and engineers are great for learning new designs. My client had another designer working on the website launch, and working with the two was a fulfilling experience to touch upon all aspects of the product launch.
