Fairchain

Iterative design for the best way to collect art.

Fairchain is a Web3 platform on desktop and mobile platforms that offers blockchain-certified contracts with resale commissions for artists within the fine art space. I was contracted as the lead product designer to design new product releases, develop their marketing site, and create responsive design systems for new and upcoming product features.

Product

Desktop/Mobile App, Art Tech, Web3

Technologies

Figma, Adobe XD, Photoshop, ReadyMag

Key Services

UX/UI Design, Wireframing, Rapid Prototyping, Web Design, Design Systems

The Problem

Fairchain has complex contracts that may alienate potential customers and the product team has upcoming product features that may add to this complication.

The Solution

Build a design system and new product features that create a unified visual message to new and returning customers to create a smooth contract-forging process.

The User

Richard, 32, Gallerist

Richard is an experienced art gallerist that is trying to use Fairchain for the first time to create a contract with an artist in an upcoming gallery show. He loves the idea of Fairchain, but he's potentially confused about certain pain points and potential complications with forging a Fairchain contract.

"Fairchain is a great concept, but I'm worried about selling the idea to other gallerists, collectors and the artist."

Motivations

  • Have clarity on how Fairchain plays into contracts for all parties
  • Complete a seamless workflow for creating a Fairchain contract
  • Integrate Fairchain into the gallery's current workflows without being jarred by the shift

Discoveries

Early Findings

  • Customers want to be highly informed on but not overwhelmed by Fairchain contracts, their various options, and more specific details.
  • Customers want to be frequently updated on the contract's process.
  • Customers need to be financially sold on and impressed by Fairchain's modern approach to the art industry.

Product Pivots

  • Create clarity through interfaces that inform but don't confuse.
  • Design supporting content such as email notifications, in-site copy, and a marketing site that help mitigate confusion for the user.
  • Design a clear design system that offers support for engineers and PMs for delivery on existing and new product features.

Iteration

Design System

Early in my contract, I realized the need for a robust and responsive design system that includes designs for desktop, tablet and mobile. Fairchain was in a period of rapid product expansion with a new feature being released every week or two. I designed a constantly updating design system that kept coherency across all platforms and created a guideline for future releases as well.

Wireframing and Component Design

Application of my design system goes hand in hand with my wireframes and component design. Creating regulated spacing, padding and margins was the first step, and I took an iterative approach to designing for new and existing features. Clarity and coherency were important to not confuse the customer, whether or not it's their first time using Fairchain for a business transaction.
