FurnishStore

Changing the way you purchase furniture.

FurnishStore is a mobile shopping platform that eases the furniture shopping process for everyone. Customers can shop through an online catalogue of local furniture products and have it seamlessly delivered to their doorstep. FurnishStore enables local businesses (family-owned, individual DIY, etc.) to sell products on an attractive, modern platform.

Product

Mobile App, E-Commerce

The Problem

College students need a platform for purchasing furniture and a way to have that furniture delivered. Local businesses or individuals need a platform that will connect them to local clients.

The User

Neha, 20, College student

Neha is preparing to move out of college dorming and start living in her own apartment. She wants to purchase furniture and build her own style, but she's worried about the logistics of buying furniture and having it delivered from somewhere other than IKEA.

"I want to buy furniture and have my own style but I don't know where it's going to be during delivery or when it's going to get here. Why can't this be like Amazon or Doordash?"

Motivations

  • Know where, when and how her furniture is going to be delivered
  • Build and develop her own style
  • Have a place to save and share her furniture

Discoveries

Early Findings

  • Customers want to be highly informed on and controlling of where their purchases are, where they will be, and how will they receive them.
  • Users will navigate through and pay attention to the top 50% of the screen.
  • Potential users are more interested in supporting local businesses than creating their own styles.

Product Pivots

  • Create highly detailed wireframes that methodically distill information through hierarchy and spacing.
  • Emphasize supporting local businesses through local product selection and subtle color choice.
  • Design a soothing yet informative purchasing experience for customers that may be experiencing their first time purchasing furniture.

Iteration

Wireframing

I proceeded to develop my information architecture and low-fidelity wireframes utilizing my user research, discoveries and user flow diagram. I prioritized building the framework and structure of the application before I implemented style guidelines and color.

Style Guide

In developing my style guide and wireframes, I constantly adjusted spacing and layout to create a soothing experience for the user, considering that for many, utilizing FurnishStore to purchase furniture might be one of their first steps in becoming independent.

Learnings

  1. Pitching an idea is like telling a short story: it hooks people in but doesn't touch every single detail. I regularly found myself iterating on my elevator pitch and dropping less important details while refining the "kicker".
  1. Reach out to experienced colleagues. They're your best allies. UX standards can be tough to remember for a junior designer, and experienced designers spot potential improvements quickly; they also have tools so you can spot them easier too.
  1. Source feedback from designers across multiple foci and industries. Feedback across the spectrum of design is a great way to think innovatively on how you can improve your own work.
