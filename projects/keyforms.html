Keyforms

An amplitude-based, hand-made audio visualizer.

Keyforms is a creative coding project I embarked on in my freshman year at NYU. It's an audio visualizer built entirely in Processing, a Java-based coding library focused on art and visual design.

The audio visualizer has several different "wavelengths" that change shape, size, and vibrancy based on the amplitude of the audio that is playing. The project was heavily inspired by oscilloscopes, which displays electronic frequencies that I tried to mimic through the Processing library.
